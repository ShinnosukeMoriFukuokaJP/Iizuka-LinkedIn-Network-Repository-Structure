## Region Overview

The **Chikuho Region** was historically the coal mining heartland of Japan, powering the country's industrialization from the Meiji era through the mid-20th century. Following the coal industry's decline in the 1960s–1980s, the region has undergone sustained economic transformation.

Today, Chikuho is positioned at the intersection of:
- **Industrial heritage** — deep manufacturing and engineering culture
- **Agricultural richness** — fertile land and food production
- **Educational infrastructure** — Kyushu Institute of Technology and regional institutions
- **Automotive manufacturing** — Toyota Kyushu in Miyawaka
- **Emerging digital economy** — AI, DX, and startup activity
