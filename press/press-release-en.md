## Unemployed Person in Post-Coal Japan Builds Nation's First Open-Source Regional Ecosystem with AI — Zero Budget

**Iizuka City, Fukuoka Prefecture, Japan** (June 2026) — Shinnosuke Mori, an unemployed resident of Iizuka City in Fukuoka Prefecture, Japan, has built and publicly launched "Iizuka LinkedIn Network" — Japan's first open-source regional professional ecosystem platform — using only free AI tools and zero budget.

#### About the Project

The platform covers the Chikuho Region of Fukuoka Prefecture (11 cities and towns including Iizuka, Tagawa, and Nogata) and functions as:

- A Wikipedia-style regional knowledge base
- A professional networking hub (LinkedIn Group)
- An AI and Digital Transformation learning hub
- A job board and startup resource center
- A career development platform

**Live site**: https://shinnosukemorifukuokajp.github.io/Iizuka-LinkedIn-Network-Repository-Structure/
**GitHub**: https://github.com/ShinnosukeMoriFukuokaJP/Iizuka-LinkedIn-Network-Repository-Structure
**LinkedIn Group**: https://www.linkedin.com/groups/14722875
**Scale**: 90+ files, 47 directories
**Budget**: ¥0 (zero)
**Built with**: Claude (Anthropic), GitHub, GitHub Pages — all free tiers

#### Background

The Chikuho Region was once the heart of Japan's coal mining industry. After the mines closed in the 1960s–1980s, the region experienced decades of economic decline. No professional ecosystem infrastructure existed for its 370,000+ residents.

Mori, currently unemployed, built the entire platform alone using AI tools. "I wanted to prove that one person with AI can build infrastructure that used to require a team and a budget," he said.

#### Why This Is Unprecedented

- No individual in Japan has built a regional knowledge base of this scale on GitHub
- No Japanese regional project has been submitted to Product Hunt and Hacker News simultaneously
- The "unemployed + AI + zero budget + post-industrial city" combination has no known precedent in Japan
