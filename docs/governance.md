# Governance

**Iizuka LinkedIn Network** · Chikuho Region · Fukuoka Prefecture · Japan

How this community is organized, how decisions are made, and how anyone can participate in shaping its direction.

---

## Governing Principles

1. **Open**: All core resources remain free and publicly accessible, forever
2. **Community-First**: Every decision serves the Chikuho Region community before any individual or organization
3. **Transparent**: Decisions and significant changes are documented publicly on GitHub
4. **Inclusive**: All contributors are welcome regardless of background, location, or technical skill
5. **Sustainable**: We build for decades, not months

---

## Organizational Structure

```
Community Members (everyone)
        ↓
  Active Contributors (regular PR submitters)
        ↓
    Maintainers (merge access, issue management)
        ↓
     Core Team (strategy, partnerships, governance)
```

### Community Members
Everyone who has starred the repository, joined the LinkedIn Group, or contributed in any way. No formal application required.

### Active Contributors  
Members who have submitted at least 3 accepted Pull Requests or made equivalent contributions (event organization, translation, etc.).

### Maintainers
Regular contributors given merge access. Responsible for:
- Reviewing and merging Pull Requests within 7 days
- Triaging GitHub Issues
- Maintaining content quality standards
- Welcoming new contributors

To become a maintainer: sustained, high-quality contributions over 3+ months.

### Core Team
Founding members responsible for:
- Overall strategic direction
- Partnership and sponsorship relationships
- Governance and policy decisions
- Community health and conflict resolution

Current Core Team: *To be published as team grows*

## Decision-Making

### Day-to-Day (No vote needed)
- Typo fixes and formatting improvements
- Adding new resource listings
- Updating outdated information
- Merging straightforward PRs

**Who decides**: Any maintainer

---

### Significant Decisions (7-day community discussion)
- Adding new repository sections
- Changing formatting or style standards
- New partnership announcements
- Policy updates

**Process**:
1. Open GitHub Issue with label `decision`
2. 7-day community comment period
3. Maintainer consensus or majority
4. Decision documented in Issue

---

### Major Decisions (30-day community process)
- Changes to mission or vision
- Monetization decisions
- Governance structure changes
- Community name or branding

**Process**:
1. Proposal in GitHub Issue + LinkedIn Group announcement
2. 30-day community discussion
3. Community vote via GitHub reactions or poll
4. Decision published in `docs/decisions/`

## Content Standards

### What We Accept
- Accurate, verifiable information
- Content relevant to Chikuho Region or regional Japan broadly
- Professional and constructive tone
- Both Japanese and English

### What We Reject
- Spam or unsolicited promotion
- Misinformation or unverified claims
- Content violating the Code of Conduct
- Off-topic content with no community value

---

## Conflict Resolution

**Step 1**: Good-faith discussion between involved parties  
**Step 2**: Escalation to a maintainer  
**Step 3**: Core team decision, published transparently  
**Step 4**: Appeals via email to [community@iizuka-linkedin-network.org](mailto:community@iizuka-linkedin-network.org)

All parties must adhere to the [Code of Conduct](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

---

## Monetization Policy

**Current status**: No revenue. Fully volunteer-driven.

**Future monetization** must:
- Never restrict access to core community resources
- Be transparently disclosed to the community before implementation
- Receive community approval through the Major Decisions process
- Align with the mission of regional revitalization

**Approved future options** (community vote required before activation):

| Type | Description | Paywall? |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Event sponsorships | Company logo/mention at events | No |
| Newsletter sponsorships | Relevant sponsor mention | Disclosed |
| Premium workshops | Optional deep-dive paid sessions | Optional (core free) |
| Corporate memberships | Company visibility and job posting | No paywall on knowledge |
| Government grants | Public funding for community projects | No |
| Affiliate links | Educational platform partnerships | Disclosed |
