## Why LinkedIn Matters More Outside Tokyo

For professionals in regional Japan, LinkedIn provides something uniquely valuable: **access to opportunities that don't advertise locally**.

- Tokyo companies hiring remote workers post on LinkedIn
- International companies entering the Kyushu market use LinkedIn
- Decision-makers at Fukuoka corporations are reachable directly
- AI and DX consultants build their entire client base through LinkedIn

**Your geographic location is an advantage**, not a handicap — if you position it correctly.

## Profile Optimization: Complete Checklist

### Photo (5 min)
- [ ] Professional headshot (not a selfie, not a group photo)
- [ ] Plain or simple background
- [ ] Business-appropriate attire
- [ ] Direct, confident eye contact
- [ ] Smile — warmth matters in Japanese professional culture

### Headline (10 min)
Your headline appears everywhere: search results, connection requests, post bylines.

**Formula**: `[What you do] | [Who you help] | [Location signal]`

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| Engineer at ABC Manufacturing | Automation Engineer · Helping Chikuho Region Factories Go Digital |
| Sales | B2B Sales · Connecting Fukuoka Prefecture SMEs with Growth |
| AI Consultant | AI & DX Consultant · Iizuka City · Helping Regional SMEs Adopt AI |

Add location keywords: `Iizuka City`, `Fukuoka Prefecture`, `Chikuho Region`

### About Section (30 min)
Structure:
```
Paragraph 1: Who you are + what you do (1–2 sentences)
Paragraph 2: Your experience and what makes you different (2–3 sentences)  
Paragraph 3: Who you work with / what you're looking for (1–2 sentences)
Line break
📍 Based in [City], Fukuoka Prefecture | Open to: [remote work / local / etc.]
📧 [email or "DM me on LinkedIn"]
```

**Include keywords naturally**: Your city, industry, and specialties.

### Experience Section
- Write descriptions in first-person, present tense for current role
- Use bullet points with **results**: "Reduced reporting time by 40% using AI automation"
- Add media: screenshots of projects, links to articles, presentations

### Featured Section
Pin your best content:
1. Your most-liked LinkedIn post
2. A link to the Iizuka LinkedIn Network repository (shows community involvement)
3. A portfolio item, article, or project

### Skills Section
Add skills relevant to your market:
- Japanese, English (if applicable)
- Your technical skills
- `Artificial Intelligence`, `Digital Transformation` if relevant

## Content Strategy

### The 3-3-1 Framework
Every week, post:
- **3 value posts** (teach something or share an insight)
- **3 engagement actions** (comment meaningfully on others' posts)
- **1 personal post** (your story, behind-the-scenes, opinion)

### Post Types That Work for Regional Japanese Professionals

**1. Local Insight**

**5. Community Resource Drop**
```
"New resource added to our Chikuho Region open-source knowledge hub:

[description of resource]

Direct link in the comments → [GitHub link]"
```

## Networking Strategy for Chikuho Professionals

### Who to Connect With
1. **Local business owners** in your city and neighboring cities
2. **Decision-makers** at companies you want to work with or sell to
3. **Recruiters** specializing in Fukuoka Prefecture or remote-friendly roles
4. **AI and DX practitioners** in Japan — build the peer network
5. **Community leaders** — Chambers of Commerce, NPOs, universities

### Connection Request Message Template
```
[Name]さん、はじめまして。
[Your name]と申します。[Your city]で[your role]をしています。

[1 specific reason you want to connect — something from their profile]

ぜひつながらせてください。

よろしくお願いいたします。
[Your name]
```

*Always personalize. Never use the default connection message.*

### The "Give First" Rule
Before asking for anything, give value:
- Comment thoughtfully on their posts (3–5 times)
- Share one of their posts with your perspective added
- Send them a useful resource with no ask attached

Then, when you reach out with a request, they already know you.

### Optimize for "Open to Work"
- Enable "Open to Work" (visible to recruiters only is fine)
- Specify job types: Remote / Hybrid / On-site in Fukuoka
